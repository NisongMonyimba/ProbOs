\section*{Month goal}
Extend the Month 4 dashboard with automotive/ISO 26262 framing,
scenario comparison, and export.

\section*{Week 18 -- Automotive framing layer}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item Largely a labeling/positioning layer, not new engineering --
    reuses the already-validated battery model exactly
  \item ISO 26262 terminology surfaced where it genuinely applies,
    without overclaiming ASIL assignment or certification
\end{itemize}

\section*{Week 19 -- Scenario comparison view}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item Compare P95 outcomes across different prior assumptions side
    by side
\end{itemize}

\section*{Week 20 -- Export functionality}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item PDF and CSV export of simulation results
  \item Exported PDFs must be genuinely traceable -- same provenance
    data as the dashboard, not a simplified summary
\end{itemize}

\section*{Week 21 -- Integration tests}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item Confirm exported PDFs contain correct, traceable numbers
    matching the dashboard's displayed results
\end{itemize}

\section*{Exit criteria}
Automotive framing is honestly presented, scenario comparison works
correctly, and exported PDFs are genuinely traceable.
